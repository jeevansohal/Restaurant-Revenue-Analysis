# Restaurant-Revenue-Analysis

## Project Overview
This project takes data from a restaurant and analyzes the revenue in regards to certain foods it sells, the times at which they are sold and the volume they are sold at. It uses a mix of MySQL to query and aggregate the data (obtained from Maven Analytics), and Tableau Public to provide visualizations to draw analyses from.

## Business Questions
1. Does revenue mix across food categories shift between meal periods, and which category drives the most revenue at each time of day?
2. What are the most impactful food items for generating revenue and minimizing loss in regards to its volume?
3. Which food items from each category are the best for revenue and by how much more than their counterparts?
4. How does total revenue compare across the months available in the dataset?

## Key Findings
- Edamame and French Fries were both underperformers when looking at quantity bought versus the revenue generated from them
- Italian and Asian dishes dominate the dinner meals and are worth putting more into since they are the most profitable
- Shrimp Scampi generates strong revenue relative to its order volume, making it an underutilized high-value menu item
## Data Source
- [Restaurant Orders Dataset](https://mavenanalytics.io/data-playground/restaurant-orders) — sourced from Maven Analytics
## Tools Used
- MySQL - data querying and aggregation
- Tableau - dashboard design and visualization
